Information About Human Trafficking


Do You Think You Know Someone In Danger?

Watch For These Possible Indicators:

  • Does the person appear disconnected from family, friends, community organizations, or houses of worship?
  • Has a child stopped attending school?
  • Has the person had a sudden or dramatic change in behavior?
  • Is a juvenile engaging in commercial sex acts?
  • Is the person disoriented or confused or showing signs of mental or physical abuse?
  • Does the person have bruises in various stages of healing?
  • Is the person fearful, timid, or submissive?
  • Is the person often in the company of someone to whom he or she defers? Or someone who seems to be in control of the situation?
  • Does the person appear to be coached on what to say?
  • Is the person living in unsuitable conditions?
  • Does the person lack personal possessions and appear not to have a stable living situation?
  • Does the person have freedom of movement? Can the person freely leave where they live?

Six Facts Everyone Should Know

Fact #1:

Human trafficking exists in every country, including the United States.

Fact #2:

Human trafficking victims can be any age, race, gender, or nationality

Fact #3:

Forced labor is also a significant and prevalent type of human trafficking.

Fact #4:

Anyone under 18 induced to perform commercial sex acts is a victim of human trafficking.

Fact #5:

Human trafficking is not the same as smuggling. “Trafficking” is exploitation-based and does not require movement across borders.

Fact #6:

Human trafficking is often a hidden crime. Victims may be afraid to come forward and get help .
